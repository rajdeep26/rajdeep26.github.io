Hello World!

Hello, World! is the Developers way of starting anything new. So I thought there couldn't be a better title than this for my first Blog post.

Hi, I am Rajdeep Mandrekar, a passionate Software Developer who loves programming so much that I can code at any hour of the day. Other than programming, I am a total Movie Buff. Be it Bollywood, Tollywood or Hollywood, it simply doesn't matter as long as the movie is good. I am a big Cricket fan (everybody loves Cricket in India. It's like a religion here), and loves Music too.

Actually I wanted to write a Blog for a very long time now. But I wasn't able to decide which Blogging Platform I should use.

  • Blogger didn't interest me much. Everybody had a Blog on Blogger, and they pretty much looked the same. Plus I wanted more control over my Blog, and my data.

  • Wordpress has grown so much that I think it will be a bit overkill to use wordpress only for Blogging. And I didn't have much time to learn and figure out how wordpress works. Plus I had to buy my own domain name and had to pay for the hosting too if I had used Wordpress.

If there were only 2 Blogging Platforms, Blogger and Wordpress, then I think I would have never created my own Blog. The reason I was interested in having my own Blog was because of Jekyll

  • Jekyll had everything I wanted in a Blogging platform. Light, Open source (which means I can customize pretty much everything), Writing blog posts in Markdown, and the main thing being, I can host it on Github Pages for free. The workflow of using Jekyll was very similar to my programming workflow. Code, Commit and Push. Simple!!

  • I was almost ready to create my Blog using Jekyll, But then I came across Ghost, another Blogging Platform. It had all the advantages of Jekyll, Plus some additional features, such as the Dashboard to create and manage posts. Moreover it looked Damn Beautiful!

I wasn't able to decide between Jekyll and Ghost. But after brainstorming for weeks and with some research, I thought of going with Ghost.

Though Jekyll acted as a catalyst in starting my own Blog, but the main reason for starting my own Blog was to share some interesting stuff I have been doing/learning at Vacation Labs. The amount of things I have learned there in last 4 months, thought me more about programming and CS, than my 4 years of Engineering.

So, this Blog will be a medium to share whatever I have learned or whatever I have been working on.

Hopefully, You'll love it.
